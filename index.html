MIRAGE — Complete Human–LLM Misalignment Statistics

The full statistical results behind the MIRAGE paper. The paper reports headline findings (e.g. 13 of 21 category mean comparisons, 18 of 21 entropy comparisons, 44 of 69 item-level mean comparisons); this page provides the complete per-comparison breakdown across all three tests, both metrics, all seven categories and 23 items.

About this page. All values are computed from the same data as the paper (8 countries × 3 LLMs × 23 WVS Wave 7 items; persona-injected, zero-shot, temperature 0.3). Countries are the unit of analysis (n = 8). Entropy uses Shannon entropy with log base 2 (bits). Only the statistical tests used in the paper are shown here — no additional tests are introduced. Values shown are the recomputed originals; the paper rounds these to two decimals.

Methods & notes

  • Unit of analysis: countries (n=8). Category- and item-level mean and entropy are the observed values.
  • Tests (exactly as in the paper): paired t-tests (df=7) for Human–LLM comparisons; signed Cohen’s dz for effect size; Friedman test (df=2) for inter-model differences with Bonferroni correction (α=.017) on pairwise comparisons. No other tests are reported.
  • Entropy: Shannon entropy, log base 2 (bits), estimated from WVS reported response proportions and sample sizes for the human baseline and computed directly from model response distributions for LLMs.
  • Scales: most items use a 1–10 scale; the three surveillance items use the WVS 1–4 scale and are compared within-item, not pooled across scales.
  • Values are the recomputed originals; the paper rounds to two decimals. Minor rounding-boundary differences in significance counts (e.g. a p just above vs. below .05) can occur; this page uses the recomputed values as canonical.
  • This appendix contains no country names or personal identifiers, consistent with double-blind review.
